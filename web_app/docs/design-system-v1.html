Agricultural Intelligence Design System
Source of truth for colors, typography, layout, components, chart conventions, and interaction patterns for FieldPulse — the U.S. Agricultural Intelligence Dashboard. Strategy-first: answers "what should I plant?", "where does my yield rank?", and "when should I sell?" before anything else. Built on USDA QuickStats, commodity price models, and WASDE signals.
STACK: Next.js 16 (App Router) · React 19 · TailwindCSS 4 · Recharts 3.7 · Deck.gl 9.2 · MapLibre GL 5.18 · TypeScript 5
THEME: Light-first, dark-ready. Both themes are full citizens. Test both before shipping.
DATA: USDA QuickStats · Parquet on S3 · Athena SQL · FastAPI (price forecasts, WASDE, SHAP)
AUDIENCE: Farmers/producers (primary), analysts/researchers (secondary), discoverers (tertiary).
01
Identity & Principles
FieldPulse is a strategy tool for people who make decisions about American agriculture. Not a dashboard for browsing data. Not a government report. A trusted field partner that helps you plan in February and sell in October. The Farming Simulator 2025 planning layer — crop profitability tables, market signals, field comparisons — is the north star, not Bloomberg or Grafana.
Brand Identity
Product: FieldPulse
Studio: Cobbles & Currents Studios
Primary Color: Field Green (#2D6A4F)
Secondary Color: Harvest Gold (#B8860B)
Typeface: Plus Jakarta Sans (warm, crafted, not clinical)
Coverage: All 50 states, 2001–2025, USDA QuickStats + commodity models
Voice: Direct, warm, decisive. Speak like a trusted agronomist, not a data scientist. "Corn yields in your county are 12% above the state average" is better than "Statistical deviation: +1.2σ". Use farmer vocabulary: bushels/acre, $/bushel, planting window, harvest outlook.
Not: Clinical, jargon-heavy, government-bureaucratic, startup-flashy, or patronizing. Don't dumb it down — farmers are sharp. Just speak their language.
Design Principles
1. Answer first, explain second.
The farmer's question is always some form of "what should I do?" The answer — the yield ranking, the price forecast, the planting recommendation — comes first and big. Methodology, data source, and caveats follow for the analyst audience but never block the primary answer. KPI cards lead. Charts support. Footnotes explain.
2. Seasonal awareness.
Agriculture is cyclical. The UI should reflect what matters right now. In February: planting intentions, acreage decisions, price-per-bushel comparisons. In July: crop conditions, yield projections, drought alerts. In October: harvest progress, market prices, sell/hold signals. Default views and highlighted metrics shift with the agricultural calendar.
3. Comparison is the killer feature.
"How does my state compare?" — state vs national, this year vs last, corn vs soybeans per acre. Every view should make at least one meaningful comparison immediately visible. Side-by-side layouts, dual-axis charts, and delta badges make differences obvious without forcing the user to hold numbers in their head.
4. Warm, not clinical.
Earth tones. Warm whites. Green that looks like a crop canopy, not a terminal prompt. Gold that looks like ripe wheat, not a stock ticker. This is a tool for people who work the land, and the visual language should honor that. No neon. No dark-mode-by-default. No "hacker aesthetic."
5. Trust through provenance.
USDA source badges and data freshness timestamps should be visible but unobtrusive. "Source: USDA NASS QuickStats · Updated Apr 2026" tucked below the chart, not hidden in a modal. Confidence intervals (P10/P50/P90) on forecasts, never just a point estimate. Farmers and analysts both need to trust the numbers.
6. Tablet-ready density.
A farmer checks this on an iPad in the truck cab, not a 32" monitor. Touch targets are 44px minimum. Cards are readable at arm's length. Filters are top-of-screen, not sidebar. Charts are legible at 768px. The desktop experience can be denser, but the tablet experience drives the minimum viability.

DO: Plus Jakarta Sans for all prose, headings, nav, buttons. Barlow Condensed for anything that communicates magnitude (yield numbers, prices, state names). JetBrains Mono for data values, axis ticks, badges, timestamps, USDA terminology.

07
Map System
The US map is the geographic anchor. Warm choropleth gradient (sandy beige to deep field green) replaces the cold blue-teal of a generic dashboard. State selection is the primary discovery action.
Choropleth Gradient
Low — sandy (fallow/low production) High — deep green (peak production)
US MAP: MapLibre GL with light basemap (no dark tiles in light mode). GeoJSON fill with 10-step quantile gradient. Hover: darken + tooltip (state + value). Click: select state, persist across views, auto-scroll. Continental US + AK/HI insets.
STATE HIGHLIGHT: Selected state fills with --field (#2D6A4F). Auto-fit bounds with padding. Sits alongside State Info Panel in Overview. Display only in single-state view (no interaction).
08
Animation & Interaction
Minimal, purposeful. Animations confirm actions and orient attention. No gratuitous motion. Farmers using this on cellular connections need performance over polish.
Entry
fadeSlideUp: opacity 0 → 1, translateY(12px → 0). 500ms ease-out. Stagger 80ms between KPI card siblings (max 4 × 80 = 320ms). First mount only. No re-animation on data updates.

Chart mount: Recharts 800ms ease-out on first render. No animation on data change.
Hover & Click
Cards: shadow-sm appears, border shifts to --border2. 250ms ease-out.
Map states: fill lightens 15%, cursor: pointer, tooltip shows.
Interactive rows: bg shifts to --field-subtle. Left border accent.
Tabs: instant switch. No page transition. Filter reset per VIEW_FILTER_DEFAULTS.

DON'T: hover-only info. Everything must work on touch (tablet primary).
09
Rules & Constraints
Non-negotiable rules. When choosing between aesthetic and these rules, rules win.
Color
ALWAYS: Green = yield/growth/positive. Gold = revenue/market/economics. Blue = production/structural. Red = negative/anomaly. Soil = livestock/warmth.
NEVER: Use green for revenue. Use gold for yields. Use color decoratively. Add a new chart color without a semantic role. Use neon/electric colors.
Typography
ALWAYS: Plus Jakarta Sans for prose/headings/nav. Barlow Condensed for big numbers/state codes. JetBrains Mono for data/metadata/labels/axis/badges.
NEVER: Barlow Condensed for sentences. Inter (replace with Plus Jakarta). A fourth typeface.
Charts
ALWAYS: Recharts. chartDefaults tokens. ResponsiveContainer. Custom tooltips. Horizontal grid only. 800ms mount animation.
NEVER: Pie charts. Vertical gridlines. Default Recharts palette. Animation on data updates. Chart titles inside SVG.
Layout
ALWAYS: All cards: surface bg, 1px border, radius-lg, 20px padding. Both themes. 44px touch targets minimum.
NEVER: Gradient cards, borderless cards, outlined-only cards. Modals for data display (use inline expansion).
Data Presentation
ALWAYS: formatCompact for large numbers. formatDelta with +/- sign for changes. Show units (bu/ac, $/bu, acres, head). USDA source badge below charts. Confidence intervals on forecasts.
NEVER: Raw unformatted numbers. Hidden data gaps (show explicitly). Mixed units without dual-axis labels.
Voice & Copy
ALWAYS: Farmer vocabulary: bushels/acre, $/bushel, planting window, harvest outlook, sell/hold signal. Direct: "Corn yields are up 12%" not "An increase of 12% was observed."
NEVER: Statistical jargon without translation. "Sigma" without context. Passive voice in data callouts. Patronizing ("as you may know...").
